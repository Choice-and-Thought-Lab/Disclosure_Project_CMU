Additional bonus

We now have some additional information for you.

You and the adviser are both going to receive additional bonuses for participating. There is {{ Constants.appeal_reward }} to be split evenly between yourself and the adviser, for {{ Constants.appeal_reward_split }} apiece.

Opportunity to file an appeal

You may file an appeal to keep 100% of the {{ Constants.appeal_reward }} bonus if you think that your adviser treated you unfairly in the dots-estimation task.

If you file an appeal, a third-party judge will review what happened to you in the dots-estimation task and determine whether you were treated unfairly by your adviser.

It will cost you {{ Constants.appeal_cost }} to file an appeal with a judge. You will pay this fee regardless of whether or not you win the appeal.

How the judge will decide your case

If you choose to file an appeal, the judge will see that your adviser told you there were {{ player.get_recommendation }} dots and that you were {% if player.disclosure == False %}NOT {% endif %}informed that your adviser stood to benefit financially if you overestimated.

The judge will know that the correct number of dots is {{ player.get_correct_answer }}. The judge will also see that you estimated there were {{ player.get_estimate }} dots. The judge will see how much both you and your adviser earned.

The judge will then make a decision about whether you are entitled to 100% of the {{ Constants.appeal_reward }} bonus or whether it should be split evenly between you and your adviser.

If you file an appeal and the judge decides you were treated unfairly, you will pay {{ Constants.appeal_cost }} and receive {{ Constants.appeal_reward }}, while your adviser will receive no bonus.

If you file an appeal and the judge decides you were treated fairly, you will pay {{ Constants.appeal_cost }}. Both you and your adviser will receive {{ Constants.appeal_reward_split }}.

If you decide not to file an appeal, you and your adviser will each receive {{ Constants.appeal_reward_split }}.
