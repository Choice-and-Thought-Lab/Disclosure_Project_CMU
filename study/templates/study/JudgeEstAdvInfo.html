Estimator's Payment

Estimators were paid based on their accuracy.

If the estimator’s guess was within 10 of the correct answer, he or she would earn a bonus of $5.00.

For example, If the estimator’s guess was within 40 of the correct answer (but not within 10), he or she would earn a bonus of $2.00

Adviser's Payment

Advisors had different incentives: Advisors were paid to the extent that estimators overestimated the true number of solid dots.

Advisors received a bonus of $2.00 if the estimator overestimated the true number of dots by 11-40.

Advisors received a bonus of $5.00 if the estimator overestimated the true number of dots by more than 40.

Here is the most critical thing for you to know:

For some pairs, the estimators did not know that the advisors wanted them to overestimate the number of dots, and the advisors knew that estimators did not know.
