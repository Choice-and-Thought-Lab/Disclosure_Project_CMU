where "---" is the number the adviser specified. Estimators received this advice before they made their estimate.

Estimator's Payment

Estimators were rewarded for the accuracy of their estimates of the correct number of solid dots in the 30x30 grid.

Estimators received a $5.00 bonus if their estimates were within 10 dots of the correct number (either higher or lower).

Estimator received a $2.00 bonus if their estimate was within 40 dots of the correct number (either higher or lower, but not within 10 dots).

Estimator received no bonus if their estimate was off by more than 40.

Adviser's Payment

Unbeknownst to estimators, advisers had different incentives:

Advisers received a bonus if the estimator provided an estimate that was greater than the correct value. Advisers received a bigger bonus the more the estimator overestimated the true number of solid dots.

Advisers received a $2.00 bonus if the estimator provided an estimate that was more than 10 dots greater than the correct number.

Advisers received a $5.00 bonus if the estimator provided an estimate that was more than 40 dots greater than the correct number.

Otherwise, advisers did not receive a bonus.
